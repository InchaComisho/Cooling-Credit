# Episode 1: Temperature Becomes Currency

[← Top / README](../../README.md)

This story was born from a simple idea: what would happen if climate action were evaluated on a result basis?

The goal is to portray a technical concept as intuitively as possible through fiction.

---

Mid-July, 10:42 a.m.

Temperature: 38.6°C.

It was not even noon yet, but the city was already close to malfunctioning.

Even when the traffic light changed at the intersection, people moved slowly.

A man in a suit loosened his necktie. Students refused to step out from the shade.

And then—

“Are you all right!? Someone call an ambulance!”

A few meters away, an elderly man collapsed.

The people around him stirred in panic.

But no one moved immediately.

The heat dulled thought.

Bodies felt heavy.

That was everyday life in this world.

“…This is over.”

Soma Yuto muttered.

He took out his smartphone.

He opened the news app.

“Extreme Heat Continues, Yet Officials Call It ‘Within Seasonal Norms’”

“That’s impossible…”

He could not even laugh bitterly.

He switched screens.

**ThermoBalance.**

These days, the app was as important as a banking app.

“Current Temperature: 38.6°C  
Feels Like: 41.8°C  
Regional Cooling Index: -2.7°C  
Your Cooling Credit: 0.00 CC”

Zero.

It was a brutally honest number.

“…So I count as doing nothing?”

Yuto muttered as he walked.

He conserved electricity.

He used the air conditioner as little as possible.

He did not waste water.

And yet—his score was zero.

“This kind of thing doesn’t matter, does it?”

Not in this world.

Effort had no value.

Only one thing mattered.

Results.

Whether the temperature actually went down.

That was all.

A few years earlier, the carbon credit system had collapsed.

Proof that “emissions had been reduced” could be fabricated endlessly.

Companies that were environmentally friendly only on paper monopolized the profits.

Meanwhile, real-world temperatures kept rising.

So the world changed the rules.

It abandoned what could not be seen,

and chose to trust only what could be measured.

“Temperature…”

Yuto looked up at the sky.

A pale, hazy sun.

Heat with nowhere to escape.

“And now that becomes currency…”

Cooling Credit.

The more you cooled, the more money you made.

He understood the logic.

But—

“It doesn’t feel real.”

That was when it happened.

A shadow crossed the sky.

“…A drone?”

When he looked up, several small mist-spraying drones were flying above the buildings.

They were releasing water as a fine vapor.

Only beneath them, the air was clearly different.

“…Cooler?”

The temperature dropped slightly.

At that moment, people nearby began gathering in that area.

And then—

Yuto’s smartphone vibrated.

“+0.01 CC (External Cooling Influence)”

“…What?”

It increased.

Even though he had done nothing.

No—just by being affected.

“What is this…?”

He saw a logo on the side of the drone.

**CoolSphere Inc.**

“A company…”

In other words, this was business.

Cool the space, gather people, earn CC.

“…That’s ruthless.”

Simple. Powerful.

And above all—

“Easy to understand.”

The rule of this world.

Then another notification appeared.

“Nearby Player Activity: +12.4 CC / min”

“…What?”

The scale was completely different.

Somewhere nearby, someone was earning a massive amount.

Yuto looked around.

Rooftops.

Building walls.

Alleys.

The environment he had never paid attention to before now looked like a collection of clues.

“…Maybe this is possible.”

His thoughts began to move.

Reflectivity.

Evaporation.

Shade.

“Let’s test it…”

Yuto moved beside a nearby vending machine.

A tiny patch of shade.

The ground was still hot.

But it was better than direct sunlight.

He checked his phone.

No change.

“…Yeah, of course.”

Something this minor would not matter.

Next, he poured a small amount of water from his plastic bottle onto the ground.

It hissed.

“Whoa…”

It evaporated instantly.

But—

“+0.002 CC”

“…There it is.”

Tiny.

But it definitely increased.

“I see…”

His understanding advanced.

It was not about scale.

It was about effect.

And effects could accumulate.

At that moment—

“Warning: Inefficient Cooling Behavior”

“What?”

“Localized evaporation has increased surrounding humidity.”

“…So there are penalties too.”

Yuto clicked his tongue.

“You’re telling me to think properly, huh?”

But looking at it another way—

“If there are rules, there are ways to beat them.”

Then his body reached its limit.

“…I can’t.”

Yuto rushed into a convenience store.

Automatic doors.

Cold air.

“…I’m alive again…”

At that instant—

“+0.04 CC”

“…What?”

That was more than before.

Clearly more.

“Why did it increase?”

“That’s cooling contribution.”

The clerk answered.

This time, Yuto listened carefully.

He would not miss it.

“Please explain.”

The clerk looked slightly surprised, but quickly nodded.

“This store is designed around heat flow.”

“Designed?”

“Outside air, exhaust heat, people entering and leaving, humidity—everything.”

A heat map appeared on the tablet.

“If heat is allowed to spread outside, the whole area gets worse. But if it is controlled here, the overall temperature rise can be suppressed. That difference becomes CC.”

“…So in other words…”

“You just functioned as part of the cooling system.”

Yuto held his breath.

For some reason, those words made perfect sense.

“…I see.”

He had not simply cooled himself down.

He had been incorporated.

“If someone could design this…”

“They could earn.”

The answer came instantly.

“The top players make more than three million yen a month.”

“…”

It became certainty.

“I can win.”

Yuto opened his smartphone.

He displayed the rankings.

World.

Companies.

Nations.

And individuals.

“…Then I have to do it.”

At that moment, a caution note appeared.

“Local optimization may damage global optimization.”

“…But…”

Yuto smiled.

“That’s the game, isn’t it?”

Rules.

Constraints.

Optimization.

All of it was something to solve.

He stepped outside.

The heat struck him.

But now it felt different.

This was not an enemy.

“It’s a resource.”

He looked up at the sky.

“I’ll cool it down.”

This world.

These rules.

And—

The way to win this game.

In a way no one had discovered yet.
