# Episode 3: Balancing Life and Cooling

[← Top / README](../../README.md)

Episode 3 follows Yuto as he tries to balance ordinary life with earning Cooling Credit.

It shows the first shape of a daily routine where work, living, and cooling must all fit into the same day.

---

8:15 in the morning.

Soma Yuto was in the back room of the convenience store, restacking boxes of rice balls.

“Put these out front at ten. Don't forget to switch the refrigerated case.”

“Understood.”

While he answered the manager mechanically, Yuto was thinking about a different set of numbers.

Hourly wage: 950 yen.

Today's shift: five hours.

Take-home pay: a little under four thousand yen.

The Cooling Credit he had earned yesterday: 0.057 CC.

Converted value: just over fifty yen.

There was still no comparison.

“…Still.”

Yuto glanced at the temperature display on the refrigerated case.

Inside: five degrees.

Outside air: already close to twenty-eight degrees.

Every time the door opened and closed, cold air and warm air exchanged places.

“This heat exchange must be calculated somewhere too.”

The feeling he had sensed yesterday at the bench and the bus stop was slowly spreading through him.

Even when he was simply working, heat was moving.

Even when he was simply arranging products, cold air was leaking.

Who gained value from that, and who suffered loss?

He could not help thinking about it.

The old ventilation fan in the back room turned weakly.

Yuto held his hand under it to feel the airflow.

“…Almost nothing.”

Heat was not escaping.

That was why, during the noon rush, the entire back room became a sauna.

Until now, everyone who worked here, himself included, had accepted it as something unavoidable.

But now it was different.

“Places people write off as unavoidable are probably where the growth potential is…”

Muttering that, Yuto took out his phone.

It would be bad to use it too much while working.

But if no one was in the back room, he could at least check briefly.

He opened **ThermoBalance** in simple mode.

Through the screen, the back room looked almost red-orange.

“Localized heat stagnation: High  
Ventilation efficiency: Low  
Cooling potential: Medium”

“Medium, huh.”

Higher than he expected.

That meant there was at least some room to earn in this corner.

“What are you doing?”

“Oh, sorry. Just checking the heat.”

He turned around and saw Takashi, a university student who worked the same part-time job.

“That cooling-something again?”

“Cooling Credit.”

“I can never remember the name.”

Takashi looked slightly exasperated, but not entirely uninterested.

“Does it bother you that much?”

“When I think there's another number besides my hourly wage, I kind of want to see it.”

“You really like numbers.”

Laughing lightly, Takashi opened the refrigerated case.

Cold air leaked out.

It spilled into the back room.

The temperature dropped ever so slightly.

Yuto's phone vibrated.

“Minor cooling influence detected: leaked cold air  
Excluded from evaluation: non-optimized cooling caused by equipment design”

“…Excluded.”

“What does that mean?”

“Looks like this is treated as wasted cold air.”

“What is that supposed to mean?”

Takashi frowned a little.

“That makes no sense.”

“It does. Even if the leaked cold air makes the back room feel cooler, the refrigerated case uses extra electricity because of it. Overall, I don't think it's counted as a cooling contribution.”

“…Ah, I get it.”

Takashi nodded.

“So feeling cool and contributing to cooling are different.”

“Exactly.”

Even this casual conversation with a coworker felt slightly enjoyable now.

Just being able to talk about the rules helped organize his own thoughts.

“So how do you make it a real cooling contribution here?”

“…Ventilation, I think.”

“Ventilation?”

“Create airflow and let the heat escape. If we reduce the heat in the back room itself, separate from the cold air leaking from the case, it might be evaluated.”

“Think it'll go that smoothly?”

“I won't know until I try.”

That phrase was already becoming half a catchphrase for Yuto.

When the morning deliveries settled down, Yuto spoke to the manager.

“Can we increase the power of this back-room ventilation fan?”

“Power? No. It's old. I'm grateful it's even turning.”

“Then the door… could we leave it open?”

“Impossible for security.”

“Right.”

The easy ideas were crushed immediately.

But he wanted to believe that the current version of himself would not stop there.

“Then…”

Yuto looked at the delivery shutter.

A faint breeze came in through the gap.

There was a temperature difference with the outside, but it was still morning.

The outside was slightly cooler.

“Could we keep the shutter half-open just during this time?”

“What about security?”

“If I'm here the whole time, I can watch it.”

The manager thought for a moment.

“Better than having someone collapse from heat…”

Muttering that, he raised the shutter lock by one level.

The gap widened a little.

Wind came in.

The air in the back room moved slightly.

Yuto's phone vibrated.

“Ventilation improvement detected  
Localized heat stagnation: slightly improved  
+0.004 CC”

“…It came up after all.”

“What did?”

“Nothing. Just talking to myself.”

The manager knew nothing.

But a single decision from him changed the comfort of everyone here, and Yuto's numbers as well.

That felt strangely real.

For the next several hours, Yuto volunteered to handle the back room.

Before the outside air became hotter, he let as much heat escape as possible.

He adjusted the shutter according to delivery timing.

It was a plain task.

But on the screen, the localized heat stagnation slowly shifted from orange toward yellow.

By the time his shift ended, the earning history was lined with tiny numbers.

“Back-room ventilation improvement: +0.004 CC  
Cumulative ventilation-efficiency improvement: +0.007 CC  
Localized heat stagnation reduction: +0.006 CC”

“Total… 0.017.”

Converted to money, it was only a dozen yen or so.

Compared with an hourly wage, it was still almost nothing.

But.

“If I can move this much while working part-time, that's not bad.”

Yuto left the convenience store and walked toward the station.

One in the afternoon.

The sunlight was beginning to grow stronger.

Reflected heat from the asphalt pushed his body temperature up from his feet.

He displayed today's total on **ThermoBalance**.

“Today earned: 0.017 CC  
Cumulative total: 0.114 CC”

“So it passed 0.1…”

As a number, it was still a small step.

But seeing the cumulative total cross into the next digit gave Yuto a quiet sense of achievement.

His rent was fifty thousand yen a month.

Electricity, gas, and water together cost more than ten thousand.

Even with food expenses cut down, life was barely manageable.

Cooling Credit alone was still nowhere near enough.

But if he kept stacking it, maybe one day it could reach something.

At the very least, he could feel that he was not doing nothing.

“…What should I do this afternoon?”

Yuto thought as he passed through the ticket gate.

The afternoon was free.

His next shift was tomorrow.

The heat was severe, but that also meant there was more room for cooling.

The sensations he had tested yesterday at the bench, the bus stop, and the bakery began forming a map in his mind.

Places where people gather.

Places where people wait.

Places where people stop.

Heat collects there.

Then he chips away at it little by little.

“Maybe the station front again…”

Just as he was about to head outside, another idea came to him.

“No. Today I'll change the route.”

If he kept looking at the same places, his perspective would become fixed.

If he walked a different path, he might find different problems.

Yuto left the station building through a back exit he rarely used.

It was a place where restaurant delivery entrances intersected with a narrow passage.

There were few pedestrians.

But heat was trapped there.

Hot air blew from restaurant ventilation outlets.

Delivery motorcycles lined the pavement, leaving residual heat from their engines.

An employee on lunch break was smoking a cigarette.

“…This is another place where someone is troubled, isn't it?”

Sweat was visible on the smoker's forehead.

He should have come out from an air-conditioned shop to take a break, but this was not a break.

Yuto opened **ThermoBalance** and scanned the passage.

“Localized heat stagnation: Medium to High  
Exhaust-heat concentration: High  
Human stay rate: Medium”

“Human stay rate: medium…”

That meant there were not a few people here.

But no one was seeing the place as a target for improvement.

Yuto looked up at the wall of the building.

Several narrow vertical windows lined it.

Some looked openable, others did not.

“…Could one of those be opened just a little?”

A staff member in an apron came out from the back door of a nearby restaurant.

“Excuse me.”

“Yes?”

“Isn't this passage hot?”

“It is, but… well, that's just how it is.”

“If there might be a way to make it a little cooler, could I test it?”

The request was sudden, but the staff member was less suspicious than expected.

“What would you do?”

“Maybe open the upper window on the wall just a little.”

“Ah… that one.”

The staff member looked up at the windows.

“We usually keep those closed. Bugs come in.”

“Bugs would be a problem.”

“But if it's only before the lunch rush… I'll ask the manager.”

A few minutes later, someone who seemed to be the manager appeared at the back door.

“Ventilation?”

“Yes. Heat is accumulating in this passage.”

Yuto recalled the notes he had made yesterday and gave a simple explanation.

“When people rest in a place where exhaust heat accumulates, local heat stress increases. If we create even a small escape route, I think the burden will go down.”

“…You say complicated things.”

The manager gave a wry smile.

“But if it's just one hour before noon, that's fine.”

“Thank you.”

They would probably need permission from the building management eventually, but today, testing was enough.

With the staff member's help, Yuto had one window opened by only a few centimeters.

At the same time, he placed a cardboard box in part of the passage, shifting the informal rest spot away from the area where exhaust heat concentrated.

The air in the passage began to move slightly.

The hot wind from the ventilation outlet escaped a little higher upward.

His phone vibrated.

“Localized exhaust-heat dispersion detected  
Stay comfort: slightly improved  
+0.005 CC”

“…There it is again.”

“Is that the Cooling Credit thing?”

“Yes.”

The staff member peered at the screen with interest.

“Does the shop get some too?”

“This one seems to be my contribution, but the overall cooling effect should also be counted for the shop.”

“In that case, maybe we should keep doing it…”

The manager's expression became a little more serious.

“It's too late after someone collapses.”

At the same time as he said that, Yuto's phone vibrated again.

“Safety improvement contribution detected  
+0.003 CC”

“So safety is a category too…”

A social side and a numerical side.

Both had moved at the same time.

In the afternoon, Yuto changed his route little by little, searching for similar hot places where someone was troubled.

The shade behind vending machines.

Lines waiting at traffic lights.

Smoking areas beside office buildings.

Each had some margin to become a little better with a small adjustment.

As a result, including the morning convenience store work, he earned 0.041 CC that day.

“Today earned: 0.041 CC  
Cumulative total: 0.138 CC”

“…The latter half of 0.1.”

Compared with the cost of real life, it was still a small number.

But it was also proof that, at an individual level, he had affected the environment that much in a single day.

That night, Yuto returned to his apartment, ate a simple dinner, and opened his notebook.

“Today's cooling actions:  
- Convenience-store back-room ventilation  
- Station-building back-passage ventilation  
- Smoking-area exhaust dispersion: not evaluated”

“Not evaluated…”

The smoking-area exhaust dispersion had not yet been evaluated by the system.

Maybe cigarette smoke and health risk were prioritized over cooling.

“Not everything becomes cooling.”

It was obvious.

But when a number forced that fact on him, it felt strangely heavy.

Yuto added a new item to the next page.

“Upper limit of CC I can move in one day:  
- Current level: about 0.04 to 0.06  
- Physical burden: Medium  
- Time burden: High”

“…So this is my current limit.”

The moment he thought that, a little emptiness began to seep in.

950 yen an hour at the convenience store.

Forty yen from cooling in the afternoon.

Neither would change his life immediately.

Both only meant something through daily accumulation.

But there was a difference.

His convenience-store wage would only increase according to rules decided by the store.

Cooling Credit could increase through his own ideas.

“…It can still grow.”

Yuto slid his finger across the phone screen.

At that moment, a notification appeared.

“A nearby notable player viewed your activity history.”

“Again…”

It was the same notification that had appeared yesterday.

This time, there was a short comment attached.

“The bench reflection sheet + ventilation combination wasn't bad.”

The sender's name was hidden.

Only the rank tier was displayed.

Silver or higher.

“…Do they have that much free time, or is this their job?”

Yuto smiled slightly.

He was moving for his own life.

Someone else was moving for the rankings.

Someone else was moving as a job.

But all of it was connected by the single point of cooling the Earth.

That was the strange and slightly hopeful part of this world.

“I'll probably talk to them directly someday…”

Muttering that, Yuto turned his phone face down.

Tomorrow's shift was in the morning.

In the afternoon, he planned to walk another route.

For now, he had not yet reached the limit.

He wanted to push this life, this cooling, and these numbers just a little further forward.
