# Episode 5: Unseen Companions

[← Top / README](../../README.md)

In Episode 5, Yuto begins to directly feel that there are other people working on cooling in the same town.

Their names and faces are still unclear, but the presence of someone fighting under the same rules slightly widens Yuto's field of view.

---

Tuesday.

As soon as Soma Yuto stepped out of his apartment, he opened his phone.

“Cumulative Cooling Credit: 0.192 CC”

“Today, I pass 0.2.”

A small goal.

But having a milestone like that made his steps feel a little lighter.

He had no part-time shift in the morning.

His next convenience-store shift was not until Thursday.

Today was completely free.

And because it was free, if he did nothing, nothing would increase.

“Station front, mall, park…”

The routes he had walked so far surfaced in his mind.

There was still room for improvement there.

But today, he strongly wanted to see a slightly different place.

Yuto turned on the “Cooling Mission” display in the map app.

Pale red and yellow markers appeared on the screen.

They indicated hotspot candidates within his living zone.

“New hotspot candidates:  
- Pedestrian bridge connecting passage  
- School route beside elementary school  
- Riverside entrance area”

“The riverside…”

About twenty minutes on foot from his apartment.

He had never looked at it as a cooling target before.

Areas along rivers should be cooler than the urban center by nature.

But depending on pavement and embankment design, heat could also accumulate there.

“I'll go see.”

Once he decided, his feet naturally headed that way.

On the way, he passed through the shopping street.

When he passed the place where he had left a note saying “It is a little cooler here” in the shade of a street tree yesterday, the paper was still there.

A tiny scribble.

But near it, one woman stood still, using her phone.

Whether she had stopped because she saw the paper, or simply because the spot happened to be shaded, he did not know.

Still, the fact that the shade was being used made him a little happy.

When he descended the slope toward the riverside, the air changed.

Wind from the river surface.

Stone embankments.

A wide sky.

At first glance, it was cooler than the town.

But underfoot was different.

The road surface near the entrance was a concrete plaza, exposed directly to sunlight.

There was a bench, but no roof.

By noon, even sitting there would probably be difficult.

“So this is it…”

Yuto opened **ThermoBalance**.

“Localized heat stagnation: Medium  
Direct sunlight exposure: High  
Human stay rate: Low to Medium”

“People probably think it's fine because the river is close, but this spot is just plain hot.”

At that moment, several people came up the riverside stairs.

A young man who seemed to be returning from a run.

A middle-aged person walking a dog.

And a woman in jogging clothes.

The woman sat on the bench near the entrance and wiped sweat with a towel.

“Hot…!”

She immediately stood up.

The bench itself had heated up so much that she could not stay seated.

Seeing that, Yuto clenched his teeth slightly.

“What a waste… this place.”

There was wind from the river.

The sky was open.

But the entrance structure alone was holding heat.

“Shade…”

He looked around.

At the edge of the entrance plaza, there was only one street tree.

But it was a little away from the bench.

The tree shade fell toward the sidewalk and did not reach the plaza.

“Could the bench be moved?”

A nearby sign said, “Do not move facility equipment.”

“Of course.”

The same line as yesterday slipped out.

“But I can move people.”

Yuto stood in the tree shade at the edge of the plaza and checked the boundary between sunlight and shadow.

Before noon, the shade would stretch a little toward the plaza.

He just needed to create a place to sit there.

Not the bench itself, but a simple seating position.

For example, a folding stool or wooden slats.

He did not have anything now.

But if he checked the situation today, he could use it for tomorrow's design.

Thinking that, Yuto scanned the temperature difference between the plaza and the shade.

“Shaded surface temperature: -3.1°C compared with surroundings  
Usage rate: Low”

“Three degrees different, and no one is using it…”

At that moment, his phone vibrated softly.

“Nearby player activity log detected  
There is a cooling proposal history near the riverside entrance.”

“Proposal history?”

It was a display he had never seen before.

“So someone already thought about something here.”

He tried to open the details, but the system rejected him with “No viewing permission.”

“Permission…”

Most likely, only players above a certain rank, or players who had cleared a certain mission, could view that log.

“But at least I know someone else thought this place was a problem.”

That alone had meaning.

Yuto took out his notebook and wrote quickly.

“Riverside entrance:  
- Plaza area is hot  
- Around 3°C difference from tree shade  
- Other player proposal history exists”

Just as he finished writing, a voice came from behind him.

“How does that spot look?”

“Huh?”

He turned around and saw a young man who looked to be in his twenties.

Running clothes, cap.

He was sweating, but his breathing was calm.

“This place was recently registered as a hotspot.”

“You know that?”

“A little.”

The young man lightly waved the smartphone in his left hand.

The screen showed the player mode of **ThermoBalance**.

For an instant, Yuto saw the rank icon.

Bronze.

“Did you try something here?”

“I did, but I haven't shaped it properly yet.”

The young man smiled a little shyly.

“Street-tree shade is nice, isn't it?”

“Shade is nice, isn't it?”

Their words overlapped by accident.

“Didn't someone tell you that the idea of making people use street-tree shade wasn't bad?”

“…They did.”

Yesterday's notification.

Someone had liked his Rest Point under the street tree.

“Could it be…”

“Could it be…”

They both started to say it, then stopped at the same time.

It was an odd silence.

But not an unpleasant one.

“Are you a player too?”

“Well, I'm still a beginner.”

“Same here.”

The young man showed his rank icon.

Bronze I.

Only a little higher than Yuto.

“Name… maybe not yet.”

“Right.”

Yuto laughed.

A stranger.

But for now, keeping this anonymous distance felt more comfortable.

“What do you think should be done here?”

The young man asked while looking over the plaza and the shade.

“Personally, I'd like to put a simple bench or wooden slats on the shaded side.”

“The issue is whether we can place anything.”

“Permission?”

“Riverside management is usually handled by the city. Going to the city office is a pretty high hurdle.”

“Right.”

Their words overlapped again.

The young man thought for a moment, then said:

“For now, I think just increasing signs that say it's okay to sit here would make a difference.”

“Signs.”

“You wrote ‘It is a little cooler here’ under that street tree, didn't you?”

“You saw that?”

“I saw it.”

The young man smiled with a slight mischievousness.

“That kind of light guidance matters.”

“Then should we do it here too?”

“Let's do it.”

Yuto took out his notebook, wrote “This is tree shade,” and placed it near the tree root.

The young man placed his own note beside it.

“Should I write ‘sitting zone’?”

“Sounds good.”

The two papers were placed side by side.

That was all.

But they might become a trigger for some people to stop there.

Yuto's phone vibrated.

“Cooperative action detected  
Shade usage rate: Slight increase  
+0.009 CC”

“Cooperative action…?”

Yuto widened his eyes slightly at the words on the screen.

“So that counts as cooperation.”

“Looks like it.”

The young man nodded while looking at his own screen.

“Probably because we made similar guidance in the same place at roughly the same timing.”

“The system is watching even that…”

“It is.”

The young man's expression became a little serious.

“The amount of CC one person can move alone has an upper limit, doesn't it?”

“It does.”

“But a cooperative action bonus is probably one way to get a little beyond that single-person limit.”

Now that he said it, it made sense.

Multiple people acting in the same place, with the same purpose.

As a result, the cooling effect spreads.

The system was evaluating that.

“Like a team…”

“Not enough to call it a team yet.”

The young man laughed.

“But I think there will be times when forming a proper team becomes necessary.”

“What kind of times?”

“When dealing with companies, or city projects. Larger-scale things.”

Those words stuck a little in Yuto's chest.

Companies.

The city.

Projects.

They still felt far away from him.

But they were probably walls he would eventually hit.

“Have you done anything like that?”

“Only a little.”

The young man smiled vaguely.

“A cooling proposal I made was adopted at the chain store where I work part-time.”

“That's impressive.”

“Whether it's impressive or not, I learned painfully well that the range an individual can affect is different from the range you can affect when you move an organization.”

The young man looked over the whole riverside plaza.

“Here too, the bench itself should really be moved. Not right in the middle of the entrance, but closer to the shade.”

“But we can't move it without permission.”

“Someone needs to propose it as an official design and get approval.”

Design.

Approval.

Official.

Yuto lined up those words in his head and checked where he stood now.

Right now, he was repeating unofficial guidance.

That had meaning.

But there were places it could not reach.

“What about you?”

“What do you mean?”

“Between what an individual can do and what it takes to move an organization.”

“…I'm still closer to the individual side.”

The young man shrugged.

“I put ideas on YouTube and social media, and talk to people little by little.”

“Social media.”

“Cooling logs and ideas. Not many people see them, though.”

For the first time, the option of “putting things out there” became concrete in Yuto's mind.

Until now, he had only used his notebook and his own screen.

He had not thought about sending anything outside.

“Sharing publicly…”

“It won't suddenly go viral, but it isn't a bad way to connect with people thinking similar things.”

The young man looked at him seriously.

“Cooling a town isn't a solo match.”

“…Right.”

Cooperative action.

Teams.

Public sharing.

It still felt a little early for him.

But he also understood that someday it would become necessary.

“Should we stop here for today?”

“Yeah.”

The two looked over the riverside entrance plaza one more time.

At the foot of the tree shade were two small notes.

Someone might see them and sit there.

On the phone screen, his progress for the day had updated.

“Today earned: 0.023 CC  
Cumulative total: 0.215 CC”

“You passed 0.2.”

“Congratulations.”

The young man said it with a light smile.

“Then you're almost at Bronze I.”

“Looks like it.”

As they parted, the young man hesitated slightly, then gave a short introduction.

“Just my handle.  
I'm WindArc.”

“…I'm Yuto.”

He hesitated over whether to give his real name, but said it anyway.

An arc of wind.

A path of wind.

The name felt strangely suited to this riverside place.

Watching the young man, WindArc, descend the stairs, Yuto quietly exhaled.

“I feel like my eyes for reading the city increased a little.”

What he could see changed.

He learned he was not alone.

That alone made the world a little thicker.

On the way back to his apartment, Yuto wrote on a new page:

“Today's observations:  
- Cooperative action bonuses exist  
- Players are working in the same town  
- This is not a solo match  
- I may eventually share my ideas publicly”

He had not yet decided to create a social media account.

But he had certainly reached the step just before that.

They are not a real team yet, and only a handle name has been exchanged, but the concept of cooperative action appearing as a number begins to widen the world beyond a solo match.

As Yuto's connections with other players increase, the way he reads and acts within the city will begin to change.

This will eventually lead toward sharing ideas through social media and open spaces, so I hope you enjoy that process as it unfolds.
