# Episode 4: Reading the City

[← Top / README](../../README.md)

In Episode 4, Yuto steps a little beyond his usual commute and begins looking at other layers of his living zone, such as a shopping mall and a small park, as cooling targets.

Even within the same town, the structure of heat changes when the place changes. This is the episode where that sense begins to rise.

---

Monday morning.

Soma Yuto stepped out through the automatic doors of the convenience store and let out a long breath.

Shift finished.

950 yen an hour, four hours.

Cooling Credit from back-room ventilation: about 0.015 CC.

The screen showed his progress for the day.

“Today earned: 0.015 CC  
Cumulative total: 0.153 CC”

“…It isn't bad, seeing the total increase little by little.”

As a number, it was still small.

But it was not zero.

Just the fact that it was not zero made him feel a little more confident toward the world.

“Now, what should I do today?”

Yuto stopped before heading toward the station.

If he walked the same route every day, he would see the usual problems more clearly.

But solving only the problems already visible would not expand his world.

Yesterday, he had cooled the back passage of the station building.

Today, he wanted to see a slightly different place.

Yuto opened the map app and zoomed in.

A little away from the station, a shopping mall and a small park stood side by side.

He rarely went to either of them.

“The mall…”

The inside would be cool.

But outside it, there should be points where exhaust heat and human traffic concentrated.

“And the park… if there are trees, then there may also be cooling seeds.”

The moment he thought that, his feet naturally moved in the direction opposite the station.

The shopping mall was about fifteen minutes away on foot.

The road along the way lay between a residential district and a main road.

The asphalt was wide.

There was a lot of traffic.

The amount of heat was not very different from the area in front of the station.

But there was a different kind of discomfort.

A small strip of tree shade stretched over part of the sidewalk.

Shade made by street trees.

But no one was standing there.

“…No one is using it.”

Yuto stopped and opened **ThermoBalance**.

“Street-tree cooling effect: Medium  
Usage rate: Low  
Cooling potential: High”

“Even if you tell me the potential is high…”

Just having the trees made the ground temperature lower than the surroundings.

But no one was there.

People troubled by the heat were not using the space.

In other words, this cooling effect was being wasted.

“…Could I move someone here?”

For now, only a few passersby were walking through.

There was no line waiting at a traffic light.

Despite the heavy traffic, few people stayed there.

Yuto left the spot as a candidate in the corner of his mind and resumed walking toward the shopping mall.

As he approached the mall, the air changed.

Near the entrance, a little cold air leaked outside.

Above the glass entrance was a large roof.

In front of it were simple benches and a smoking area.

“A smoking area again…”

The same structure as yesterday existed in another place.

A space where exhaust heat, smoke, and people gathered.

By noon, this place would also become hot.

Yuto stood beside the mall entrance and scanned the whole area.

“Localized heat stagnation: Medium  
Exhaust-heat concentration: Medium  
Human stay rate: Medium to High  
Cooling potential: Medium”

“Everything is medium…”

But Yuto knew that this kind of “all medium” was exactly where growth potential existed.

If a place was truly terrible, someone would already have taken measures.

His job was the slightly troublesome places no one was looking at.

Several office workers were standing in the smoking area.

One had taken off his jacket and slung it over his shoulder.

Another had a cooling towel around his neck.

“It's hot…”

“How long is this going to last?”

Their conversation sounded detached.

But the heat was certainly cutting into their lives.

Yuto noticed a small covered bench at the edge of the smoking area.

There was a roof.

But the angle was bad.

During hours when the sun entered diagonally, the shadow missed the bench.

“…Could the roof angle be changed?”

He could not touch it without permission.

But he could consult the mall management office.

Yuto entered the mall and looked at the information board.

He found the management office, but hesitated to walk in suddenly.

“It's pretty brave, interfering with someone else's facility when I'm not even working…”

Even while thinking that, his feet gradually moved toward the office.

When he knocked on the door, a man who looked like an office worker came out.

“Yes?”

“Um, about the smoking area outside and the roof over the bench…”

“The roof?”

“Is there any possibility of adjusting its angle a little?”

The man looked suspicious.

“What do you mean?”

“I think changing the fall of the shade and the way exhaust heat accumulates could reduce the heat burden on people staying there.”

His explanation became somewhat abstract.

But the man did not dismiss him completely.

“Cooling Credit?”

“Yes.”

The moment Yuto said that, the atmosphere changed slightly.

“We have been getting more consultations like that lately.”

The man returned to his chair and operated a terminal as he continued.

“Individuals come in asking whether there are points in our facility where they can contribute to cooling.”

“So there really are people doing that.”

“There are. But we cannot accept every proposal.”

Of course.

Safety, cost, specifications.

The management side had its own circumstances.

“For now, shall we display the recommended shade pattern for the outside smoking area?”

The man opened the mall's cooling management screen.

It displayed a heat map of the entire facility and recommended settings.

“Facility Cooling Management Mode  
Smoking Area: Current Setting = Standard  
Recommended Setting = Shade Priority + Ventilation Assistance”

“Shade priority?”

“A mode that prioritizes shaded areas.”

“You can change it?”

“We can change it at management discretion. The issues are the satisfaction of smoking-area users and the impact on other areas.”

The man looked at Yuto briefly.

“If you like, we can log your proposal as a reference.”

“Proposal?”

“What angle you imagine, what kind of shade fall you expect, and where you want people to stand.”

“…Where I want people to stand.”

Yuto reconstructed the smoking area in his mind.

Shade.

Exhaust heat.

Wind.

“I want the roof shade to overlap the bench and the smoking space as much as possible, and I want to prevent exhaust heat from staying along the wall.”

“Specifically?”

“Have the roof angle tilted a little more toward the south than it is now, changing where the shade falls. Also, place a small standing-position marker in front of the vent where exhaust heat is strongest, so people naturally gather a little away from it.”

The man typed it into the terminal while nodding.

“Hmm…”

A few minutes later, a simple simulation result appeared on the screen.

“Proposal Pattern:  
Localized heat stagnation: Slight decrease  
Stay comfort: Slight improvement  
Impact on other areas: Minor  
Expected cooling contribution: +0.012 to +0.017 CC/day”

“Not bad.”

“It gives an expected value?”

“This system was updated recently. It can roughly evaluate outside proposals before trying them.”

“Then…”

“If smoking-area users do not seem likely to complain, we will test the setting for one week.”

“Thank you.”

When Yuto left the office, some of the tension left his shoulders.

His proposal had been incorporated into a facility setting screen.

That itself carried a different weight from anything before.

He went outside the mall and looked at the smoking area again.

The roof angle had not changed yet.

The setting would probably be adjusted at night or the next morning.

“I'll come check tomorrow.”

He had gained something to look forward to.

Leaving the shopping mall, Yuto headed to the park.

The park was smaller than he had expected.

Play equipment, benches, a few trees.

A sandbox and a small fountain.

“A fountain…”

Water was a seed of cooling.

But if used incorrectly, it only raised humidity.

Yuto walked around the fountain and checked the positions of benches and tree shade.

By midday, the fountain water became lukewarm.

The surrounding stone pavement stored heat.

But a little farther out, cool places remained.

“This place also has locations where someone is troubled, and cool places no one is using.”

Children playing beside the sandbox were under direct sunlight.

Depending on the time of day, the bench shadows did not reach the sandbox.

“Could the bench be moved?”

He looked at the park management sign. It said, “Do not move benches.”

“Of course.”

Yuto let out a laugh that was half sigh.

“But maybe I can change where people stand.”

If he placed a small marker near the sandbox, the children might shift their play area slightly.

The parents might change where they sat too.

That day, Yuto did nothing large in the park.

Instead, he decided to create a simple “rest point” for entering the shade.

He placed a small paper in the tree shade that said, “Rest Point.”

To anyone looking closely, it was almost like a scribble.

But children and parents often noticed things like that.

As a result, that afternoon, a few more people gathered in the park's shade than usual.

Some of the people clustered near the fountain moved toward the shaded side.

His phone vibrated.

“Shade usage rate improvement detected  
Stay comfort: Slight improvement  
+0.006 CC”

“…It really is about human movement.”

In other words, benches, fountains, and trees were only cooling seeds.

How those seeds were used.

Who stood where.

That became value.

In the evening, on his way back to the apartment, Yuto passed the sidewalk with the street trees again.

As in the morning, the shade had stretched out.

But there were still few people stopping there.

“…Maybe I'll put a rest point here too.”

Yuto took a small notebook from his pocket and wrote, “It is a little cooler here,” then quietly placed the paper near the root of a street tree.

It was not an official sign.

But it could still become a trigger for someone to notice that this place was different.

The Cooling Credit he earned that day from the mall, park, and sidewalk adjustments added up to 0.039 CC.

“Today earned: 0.039 CC  
Cumulative total: 0.192 CC”

“0.2 is coming into sight…”

Back in his apartment, Yuto opened his notebook.

“Today's observations:  
- When a proposal goes through a facility management screen, it becomes official  
- Cooling seeds such as trees, fountains, and roofs are everywhere  
- The problem is how people use them”

As he wrote, his pen suddenly stopped.

“The range I can move alone is at most one part of this town…”

Fatigue slowly pressed in.

Convenience-store shifts.

Travel time.

Cooling actions.

Even using the whole day, he could move only around 0.04 CC.

His physical limit was beginning to approach.

“Someone else must be doing similar things too.”

Yesterday's notification.

And the one that slid down from the top of the screen again today.

“A nearby player liked your Rest Point log.”

“…Liked?”

It was the first notification of that type he had seen.

When he opened the details, only one comment appeared.

“The idea of making people use the shade of street trees is not bad.”

As always, the sender's name was hidden.

Only the rank tier was displayed.

Bronze or higher.

“Not Silver…?”

It was a different tag from the notable player notification he had seen before.

“So that means there really are people in the same town doing similar things.”

That felt strangely encouraging.

He could not cool the whole town alone.

But if more people moved the way he did, the story would change.

The moment he thought that, Yuto added a new item to his notebook.

“Possibility of forming a team someday:  
- People acting in the same living zone  
- People connected to facility management  
- Professionals who design cooling systems”

“I'm not there yet.”

For now, he was still barely able to move his own step forward.

But at the edge of his vision, the shadows of other players had certainly begun to appear.

The town was wide.

Heat was wide.

And the methods for cooling were wide as well.

Looking at the cumulative total of 0.192 CC, Yuto quietly exhaled.

“Tomorrow, I'll pass 0.2.”

That small goal would not dramatically change his life.

But it was enough of a reason to keep moving his days forward.
