# Episode 6: Should the Logs Go Outside?

[← Top / README](../../README.md)

In Episode 6, Yuto begins to think about putting his cooling logs and ideas outside of himself.

Through his part-time work, the city, and conversations with other players, he takes a small step from being someone who merely records to someone who might share.

---

Wednesday.

Soma Yuto felt slightly restless from the morning.

The words of the player he had met the previous day at the riverside, WindArc, kept lingering in his mind.

“Cooling a town is not a solo match.”

That one sentence felt strangely heavy.

His convenience-store job.

Calculating living expenses.

His daily cooling actions.

Until now, he had seen all of them as things that were completed inside himself.

But other players already existed.

Facility management screens had also begun preserving his proposals as logs.

In that situation, the question naturally surfaced: should he put his own logs outside too?

“…Social media.”

While biting into toast for breakfast, Yuto stared at his phone.

Create new account.

Set profile.

Choose icon.

Post.

If he wanted to start, it would take only a few minutes.

If he did not want to, he could spend his entire life never starting.

“It feels too early, but it also feels like I should start soon.”

Both feelings were equally strong.

For today, he decided to go through his daily routine while thinking about what he would write and how he would write it if he were to share something.

In the morning, Yuto passed through the shopping street and headed to work.

Today was not the convenience store, but a different one-day job: warehouse sorting.

The hourly wage was slightly higher.

But the environment was harsh.

The warehouse was wide.

The ceiling was high.

But the air conditioning barely worked.

“It's hot…”

Yuto put on work gloves and looked around.

Pallets.

Cardboard boxes.

Forklifts.

People.

Heat sources were everywhere.

Even so, it was a space where everyone accepted it as unavoidable because it was hot.

When he opened **ThermoBalance** in simple mode, the entire screen turned dark orange.

“Localized heat stagnation: High  
Ventilation efficiency: Low to Medium  
Cooling potential: Medium”

“Medium…”

Cooling the entire warehouse as an individual was obviously impossible.

In many cases, places like this would become targets for large corporate projects.

“But I can at least adjust the flow line.”

Yuto looked toward his assigned area: the picking aisle.

Long shelves stood in rows, and people moved between them.

The aisles were barely wide enough.

Almost no wind passed through.

“Could I make even part of this into an escape route?”

During a break, Yuto casually spoke to the warehouse employee in charge.

“Isn't this aisle hot?”

“It is.”

“If I could make the airflow a little better, could I test it?”

“What would you do?”

“I would shift the temporary storage area slightly and create a path for air to pass through.”

The employee thought for a moment.

“If you can keep it safe, test only a small section.”

“Thank you.”

Yuto shifted the temporary storage space at the end of the aisle slightly.

It was not a full layout change, only enough to increase a gap where people could pass more easily.

The airflow changed faintly.

The way heat accumulated also dispersed a little.

His phone vibrated.

“Aisle ventilation improvement detected  
Work comfort: Slight improvement  
+0.008 CC”

“It still appears.”

Looking at that result, Yuto suddenly thought:

“If I posted something like this on social media, how would I write it?”

Warehouse aisles.

Cargo placement.

Airflow.

He could make it a diagram.

Or a simple illustration.

If he posted something with a title like “Three ways to make warehouse aisles cooler,” it might help someone else's workplace.

But at the same time, he felt anxious.

“How many people actually want this kind of thing?”

An account with zero followers.

A first post.

The possibility of no reaction.

Was it still worth doing?

He was not confident yet.

In the afternoon, when he finished work and stepped outside, the sky was already hazy white.

Temperature: thirty-three degrees.

Feels like: higher than that.

Yuto chose a route home different from yesterday's.

Instead of passing the shopping mall, he took the road beside the elementary school.

There was a short pedestrian bridge along the school route.

Children used it to avoid direct sunlight on their way from the school gate toward the station.

But the entrance area of the bridge had almost no shade.

The stairs took direct sunlight, and the handrails held heat.

By early afternoon, touching them could almost burn a hand.

“Here too…”

Yuto stopped under the pedestrian bridge stairs and scanned it.

“Localized heat stagnation: Medium  
Direct sunlight exposure: High  
Pedestrian age group: Low to Medium  
Cooling potential: Medium”

“Children use this, and it's like this…”

He could not ignore it.

What he could do immediately was limited.

But at the very least, guiding people away from using it during dangerous hours was something an individual could do.

Yuto checked the side path next to the bridge.

It was a detour, but it had more shade.

“I'll put a marker for the detour.”

On the spot, Yuto wrote on a note, “On hot days, there is also a shaded route this way,” and placed it at the entrance to the side path.

It was not an official sign.

But if someone saw it, it would increase their choices.

His phone vibrated.

“Danger-avoidance guidance detected  
Safety contribution: Slight improvement  
+0.007 CC”

“Safety appeared again…”

The same as the riverside entrance yesterday.

Cooling was not only about lowering temperature.

It also included moving people exposed to heat away from dangerous environments.

“This is exactly the kind of thing that should be shared.”

Thinking that, Yuto suddenly stopped.

What would happen if he posted this action with photos or diagrams on social media?

Someone in a town with a similar school route might imitate it.

That was certainly valuable.

But at the same time, it meant exposing his own actions to other people's eyes.

“Exposing myself…”

In a good sense and a bad sense.

Placing a note on a school route without permission could itself make someone angry.

If he posted it online, both agreement and criticism would increase.

“Do I have that much resolve right now?”

He could not answer that question yet.

In the evening, after returning to his apartment, Yuto placed his phone on the desk and stared at it for a while.

New account creation screen.

Username.

Profile.

His finger moved as far as the name field.

But he did not type.

“…I'll stop for now.”

Today, he would only think.

Instead, Yuto decided to organize the logs inside himself.

He opened his notebook and made a simple list of his cooling actions so far.

“Yuto Cooling Log (draft):  
- Convenience-store back-room ventilation  
- Station-building back-passage ventilation  
- Bus-stop waiting-line shade guidance  
- Bakery-front mist and awning observation log  
- Riverside entrance tree-shade guidance  
- Shopping-street street-tree rest point  
- Warehouse aisle ventilation improvement  
- School-route danger-avoidance guidance”

When lined up, they somehow looked like a genre of their own.

“Just organizing them like this clears my head a little…”

Whether he put them outside or not, he could organize them inside himself.

That too was a proper step.

His phone vibrated.

“System Notice  
Your cooling logs have exceeded a certain number.  
Personal Cooling Report function has been unlocked.”

“Report function?”

A new button appeared.

“You can summarize your cooling actions and share them with other players.  
Visibility: Private / Nearby players only / Public”

“…I see.”

This was a little different from social media.

It was a sharing function inside **ThermoBalance**.

Instead of sending it into the outside world, he could share cooling logs within the same system.

He could limit the visibility.

In other words, it was not fully open, but semi-public.

“With this…”

Some of the tension left Yuto's shoulders.

He did not need to broadcast to the whole world immediately.

First, he could try summarizing his logs for nearby players only.

That kind of step felt like something his current self could take.

He opened the report creation screen.

After hesitating over the title field, he typed:

“Small Cooling Logs in Daily Life”

In the description field, he wrote briefly:

“Notes on cooling actions that can be done alongside part-time work and commuting.  
They are not large-scale projects, but examples of what an individual can still move.”

For visibility, he selected “Nearby players only.”

Someone acting in the same town, like WindArc, might see it.

Someone in the Silver tier might find it useful.

That much, he felt, he could accept.

When he pressed the submit button, a small notification appeared.

“Cooling report published  
Visible to: Nearby players (Bronze and above)”

After a while, another notification arrived.

“A nearby player reacted to your cooling report.”

“That was fast…”

When he opened the details, one short comment was displayed.

“Logs from the perspective of daily movement routes are useful.”

The sender's name was hidden.

Rank tier: Silver or higher.

“…So they really are watching.”

The unseen gaze he had felt at the riverside yesterday now had a slightly clearer outline.

He had not created an outside social media account yet.

But he had begun semi-open sharing within the system.

For him, that was a major change.

The Cooling Credit he earned that day from the warehouse and school-route actions totaled 0.036 CC.

“Today earned: 0.036 CC  
Cumulative total: 0.251 CC”

“0.25…”

One quarter.

A quarter of one.

It was not tied to any particular unit, but having that kind of milestone made him a little happy.

At the end of the notebook, Yuto added one line for the day.

“Today's observations:  
- I don't have to share with the whole world right away  
- I can begin with semi-public sharing inside the system  
- When logs go outside, someone somewhere is watching”

He had not made an outside social media account yet.

But he had reached the step just before it.

Keeping that distance, Yuto quietly closed his notebook.

Thank you for reading Episode 6.

This time, Yuto began thinking about whether to put his cooling logs outside of himself.

Rather than suddenly broadcasting to the whole world through social media, he takes a more natural first step through a semi-public “nearby players only” report inside **ThermoBalance**.

By taking this stage first, the story can naturally connect later to sharing ideas on social media and open-license-like systems.

I hope you continue to enjoy seeing how Yuto's perspective expands.
