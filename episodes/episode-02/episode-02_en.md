# Episode 2: The First Cooling Income

[← Top / README](../../README.md)

In Episode 2, Yuto tests for himself whether it is truly possible to earn in this world.

The larger conflict has not begun yet. This chapter focuses on the pleasure of thinking, trying, and seeing numbers come back as proof.

---

The next morning, Soma Yuto woke up before his alarm rang.

He was short on sleep.

To be exact, he had barely slept at all.

The reason was simple.

Yesterday's 0.04 CC.

That tiny number had stayed in his head all night.

It was only a fractional reward displayed on his phone screen.

It was not a large amount of money.

But its meaning was enormous.

If you cool, you can earn.

The moment Yuto realized that this rule truly applied to him, the way he saw the world changed.

“…It wasn't a dream, right?”

He picked up the smartphone beside his pillow.

He opened **ThermoBalance**.

“Cooling Credit Balance: 0.04 CC  
Estimated Value: 39 yen”

“It didn't disappear…”

He felt a little relieved by something that should have been obvious.

Yuto sat up.

His cheap apartment was a single six-tatami room.

There was an old air conditioner, but he had barely used it over the past few days.

If he used it, the electricity bill would be frightening. More than that, some part of him had believed there was meaning in enduring the heat.

But yesterday, he understood clearly.

Endurance itself had no value.

The world looked only at outcomes.

“Then…”

Yuto let out a small breath.

“I have no choice but to answer with results too.”

He opened the window.

Even though it was morning, the air was already lukewarm.

From the distant road came the low engine sound of delivery trucks.

The balcony railing was already warm under the morning sun.

Yuto opened the notes he had written the previous night.

Before sleeping, he had typed down ideas in a rough list.

“Ways to cool:  
- Sprinkle water  
- Make things white  
- Increase shade  
- Let wind pass through  
- Release heat  
- Guide people toward cooler places”

It looked like an elementary school science project.

But for now, that was enough.

“The problem is what gets properly evaluated.”

“…Before that.”

Yuto looked at the shopping bag beside his bed.

He had bought the items yesterday on the way home, half out of desperation.

Cooling sheets for fever.

A cooling band worn around the neck.

A small handheld fan.

And a slightly more expensive portable fan labeled “with cooling plate.”

All of them were standard summer products piled up in stores.

“Normally, you'd think using this stuff would increase your CC, right?”

As if telling himself so, Yuto first placed a cooling sheet on his forehead.

A cold sensation spread across his skin.

“Oh… this definitely cools.”

He immediately opened **ThermoBalance**.

“Personal surface-temperature reduction detected  
Estimated user return: +0.001 CC  
Product cooling contribution attributed to registered manufacturer account”

“…Huh?”

Yuto looked at the screen again.

User return.

Manufacturer attribution.

In other words, the system recognized that cooling had occurred.

But most of the value was counted toward the side that created the product.

“Well, I guess that makes sense… I'm not the one doing the cooling. The product is.”

He could understand it.

Still, it was frustrating.

Next, Yuto wrapped the cooling band around his neck.

It did make him more comfortable.

His breathing felt slightly easier.

But the display was similar.

“Wearable cooling assistance detected  
Estimated user return: +0.001 CC  
Development and supply-chain contribution will be prioritized”

“…Barely anything.”

On top of that, the bottom of the screen showed a small cost-effectiveness notice.

“Estimated purchase-cost recovery rate: Low”

“Helpful, but merciless…”

In other words, commercial products were excellent as commercial products.

They cooled properly.

They improved comfort.

But in the world of CC, a person who merely bought and used them did not keep much of the profit.

The company that developed it.

The side that designed it.

The side that distributed it.

The side that supplied it in large quantities and spread cooling effects through society.

More credit flowed to them.

“So the user gets a small benefit, and the maker and seller earn properly.”

It was well designed, he thought.

A resale-style shortcut would not work.

Buying something and stopping there would not win.

At least in that area, this world was strangely precise.

Finally, Yuto turned on the item he had expected the most from: the handheld fan with a cooling plate.

A small motor hummed.

The plate section was certainly cold.

It felt good against his neck.

“This one's got to be a hit.”

He looked at the screen.

But the message that appeared was unexpected.

“Localized cold sensation detected  
Total cooling contribution: 0.000 CC  
Reason: waste heat and electricity use exceed perceived comfort improvement”

“Eh?”

Yuto froze.

“Zero?”

He looked again.

It was not a mistake.

“Notice: Some portable cooling devices improve perceived comfort but do not match environmental cooling.”

“…Seriously?”

It was cold.

But it was not cooling.

At least, that was how the system judged it.

His skin felt better.

But to achieve that, heat was pushed elsewhere, electricity was consumed, and overall the surroundings were not cooled.

In some cases, they might even be made slightly worse.

“So comfort and cooling are different…”

The moment he thought that far, everything suddenly made sense.

The CC he had received at the convenience store yesterday had not been because he personally felt cool.

It had been because the store's entire heat flow was optimized, and he had functioned as part of that system.

In other words.

Just buying something cold was not enough.

Just feeling comfortable was not enough.

To earn in this world, he had to change the flow of heat itself.

“…I see.”

Yuto lined up the cooling goods on his desk.

None of them were useless.

As tools to survive the heat, they had value.

But they would not make him the one who earned.

“Commercial products are tools to use, not the answer for winning.”

The moment he said that, something switched in his mind.

What he needed was not to consume ready-made goods.

He had to find things himself.

Combine things himself.

Read the unevenness of heat and devise small interventions himself.

Otherwise, Cooling Credit would not grow.

“Then…”

Yuto opened last night's note again.

“Ways to cool:  
- Sprinkle water  
- Make things white  
- Increase shade  
- Let wind pass through  
- Release heat  
- Guide people toward cooler places”

Now the list looked far more meaningful than before.

“The problem is what gets properly evaluated.”

Using commercial goods alone did not work. The rough water-sprinkling he had tried yesterday had produced a tiny amount of CC, then immediately triggered a warning.

If he only caused localized evaporation, humidity could rise and make the result worse.

That meant this game could not be won with simple goodwill.

What mattered was effect.

And preferably, continuing effect.

“Not just cooling something once… but maintaining cooling with little effort?”

When he said it out loud, it sounded a little like strategy.

Yuto opened the refrigerator.

He drank a mouthful of barley tea and bit into the cheap bread he had bought the day before.

It was less breakfast than fuel.

Even while eating, possible options lined up in his mind.

Water sprinkling.

Shade.

Reflection.

Airflow.

Plants.

“…Plants are impossible for now.”

He could not prepare them immediately.

“Making things white, huh…”

His eyes moved to the corner of the room.

A foldable white leisure sheet.

He had bought it the previous year for a river outing that never happened, and had never used it even once.

“Could this work as a reflector?”

It would reflect sunlight.

If he could reduce heat storage in the ground or walls even slightly, maybe the system would recognize it as a cooling effect.

Of course, it might not work that conveniently.

But by yesterday, Yuto had already learned one thing.

In this world, the fastest way was to test whatever came to mind.

Yuto took the leisure sheet and looked around the room.

He also put a small USB fan, an empty plastic bottle, and a notebook into his bag.

“All right.”

He muttered to no one and opened the front door.

Outside, it was already hot.

A little past eight in the morning.

Normally, it should still be a heat one could endure.

But in this city, needing endurance at eight in the morning was already wrong.

As he descended the apartment's outside stairs, his phone vibrated.

“Today's Regional Mission: Improve heat stagnation within your living zone by 0.5%.”

“Regional mission…?”

When he tapped it, a simple map appeared.

The block where Yuto lived was stained pale red.

“Building density: High  
Shade ratio: Low  
Road-surface heat storage: High  
Recommended actions: Reflection, ventilation, evapotranspiration”

“…This is way too much like a game.”

He laughed without meaning to.

But it was not really something to laugh about.

The recommended actions matched his items surprisingly well.

“Reflection and airflow, huh.”

As Yuto walked through the alley, he looked around.

A narrow residential area.

Low buildings packed close together, blocking airflow.

Bicycles along the road.

Hot air from outdoor air-conditioner units.

Every time he turned a corner, thick warm air clung to him.

“It feels like the town itself is holding heat…”

Until yesterday, he would have dismissed the scenery as simply hot.

Today, all of it looked like a structure of problems.

Where did the exhaust heat go?

Where was the shade concentrated?

Where did the wind stop?

As he walked while thinking, he saw a small coin laundry at the end of an alley.

In front of it was a bench no one was using.

Right above it, morning sunlight reflected from the wall and made the area unnaturally bright.

“…Here?”

Yuto stopped.

The wall was pale cream, but darkened in places and looked like it would trap heat.

The bench was made of metal, the kind that would be impossible to sit on by noon.

Above all, there was no wind.

It was almost a dead end, a place where only heat remained.

Yuto opened **ThermoBalance** and started simple scan mode.

Through the screen, that corner appeared a slightly deeper red.

“Localized heat stagnation: Medium  
Improvement potential: Available”

“Available, huh.”

It was vague, but it pushed him forward.

First, Yuto spread the white leisure sheet behind the bench, along the wall where the morning sun hit most strongly.

He had no tape, so he fixed it roughly to a nearby metal fence and pipe using clothespins and string.

It looked extremely suspicious.

“…I won't get reported, right?”

He felt a little uneasy.

But for now, no one was watching.

Next, he connected the USB fan to a mobile battery.

The airflow was weak.

But maybe even moving the stagnant air a little would make a difference.

Finally, he filled the empty plastic bottle with a small amount of water from a nearby faucet and spread it very thinly on the ground in front of the sheet.

He remembered yesterday's mistake.

Do not splash carelessly.

Spread it widely and thinly so it would not evaporate too quickly.

“…Will this really change anything?”

Saying it himself made him more anxious.

He stared at his phone.

A few seconds.

Ten seconds.

Twenty seconds.

No change.

“Yeah, figures…”

It was not that easy.

Maybe only companies and municipalities with real equipment could make a difference.

Maybe an individual making small adjustments in an alley would not even be evaluated.

Just as he thought that—

“Minor cooling influence detected  
+0.006 CC”

“…Oh.”

It increased.

Only a little.

But it increased.

“It appeared…!”

His voice slipped out.

Small.

Very small.

But this was not like yesterday, when he had happened to enter a store.

He had thought it up, placed it himself, and caused the change himself.

Yuto quickly opened the details screen.

“Contribution factors: Reflection 42%  
Ventilation assistance 21%  
Surface-temperature reduction 19%  
Evaporation 18%”

“…It breaks it down properly.”

If the system showed that much, the rest was easy.

What worked, and what was weak.

Which actions were wasteful, and which could grow.

In other words, he could improve.

“This is… fun.”

Before he realized it, his cheeks had loosened.

At that moment—

“Warning: Unconfirmed installed object detected.”

“Whoa, there it is.”

“Public pathway obstruction risk: Low  
Local consent recommended”

“Consent…?”

As Yuto looked at the screen, a voice came from behind him.

“Did you do that?”

He turned around.

A woman holding a laundry basket was standing there.

She looked to be in her thirties.

Probably a customer of the coin laundry.

“Ah, sorry. If it's in the way, I'll remove it immediately.”

“It's not exactly in the way…”

The woman looked at the white sheet.

Then she placed a hand on the bench and widened her eyes slightly.

“Oh. It's not as hot as usual.”

“Huh?”

“This bench usually gets too hot to sit on before noon.”

As she said that, she sat down.

“Today it's fine.”

At the same time, Yuto's phone vibrated.

“Improved usability comfort detected  
+0.011 CC”

“It went up…!”

“What did?”

“Ah, nothing. Just talking to myself.”

Yuto hurried to cover it up.

But internally, that was huge.

It was not just cooling.

There was value in making a place usable for people.

The system might be looking not only at temperature differences, but also at whether the environment could actually be used.

The woman smiled a little.

“People doing this kind of thing are more common lately, aren't they? Making little improvements to earn CC.”

“So there really are people doing it.”

“Of course. Electricity and food are both getting more expensive.”

It was an extremely realistic answer.

Yuto gathered a little courage and asked.

“Is there anyone around here who's good at it?”

“Not exactly a master, but the bakery near the station is famous. The front of the shop feels cool, doesn't it?”

“…It does.”

“They're apparently good at the mist direction and the shade angle. On hot days, everyone stops there.”

People gather.

They stay.

The cooling effect spreads.

And CC accumulates.

Structurally, it was the same as the corporate drones he had seen yesterday.

Only the scale was different.

“Thank you.”

“No problem. I thought you were doing something weird, but it helped a little.”

The woman said only that and entered the laundry.

After watching her go, Yuto looked at the screen again.

Total: 0.017 CC.

Converted to money, it was only a dozen yen or so.

Honestly, not enough to change his life.

But.

“This can work.”

This time, he felt it more clearly than yesterday.

There was reproducibility.

It was not coincidence.

If there were rules, he could learn them.

Yuto opened his notebook on the spot and wrote quickly.

“If people can use the place, the bonus is stronger?  
Ventilation + stayability matters more than reflection alone  
Small combined effects grow better than isolated effects”

“…I see.”

In this game, optimizing combinations was stronger than leveling a single skill.

Making something white alone was weak.

Sending wind alone was weak.

But in a place where heat stagnated, combining reflection, airflow, and a little evaporation produced numbers.

It was almost like—

“A build.”

Skill composition.

Synergy.

Conditional multipliers.

Suddenly, the world looked familiar.

It was not simply an environmental problem or a social system.

At least for Yuto, it was starting to become an攻略able system.

Then another notification appeared.

“Tutorial Updated  
Next Objective: Achieve 0.05 CC within your living zone”

“0.05, huh…”

Even with what he had just earned, he was still short.

But it was not impossible.

Yuto left the alley and headed toward the station.

He was curious about the bakery the woman had mentioned.

As the station street approached midday, more people appeared.

But people were not the only thing increasing.

Heat, noise, and irritation swelled together.

There was a line at the taxi stand.

Students and elderly people crowded under the bus stop roof.

In places where building winds did not pass, merely standing still felt suffocating.

“Places like this probably have the answer…”

He saw a small bakery ahead.

A deep awning covered the front of the shop.

Several customers stood beneath it.

Fine mist drifted from the wall side.

And it was not simply spraying randomly.

The angle was adjusted so it flowed toward the downwind side.

“…That's it.”

He could tell just by getting close.

It was slightly cooler than the surroundings.

Not the artificial coldness of air conditioning, but the soft coolness of stepping into tree shade.

Yuto stopped and opened **ThermoBalance**.

“Surrounding cooling optimization: High  
Stay comfort: High  
Registered cooling-contribution business: Yes”

“Business registration…”

This was not an individual's game.

The store was running it as a real mechanism.

“Welcome!”

A bright voice came from inside.

Yuto entered almost reflexively.

The shop was small, but clearly easier to breathe in than outside.

It was not overcooled.

But heat did not stagnate.

That delicate balance impressed him.

“…Amazing.”

“Right?”

The woman at the register smiled.

She looked to be in her early twenties.

Her hair was tied short, her eyes looked cool, and the bakery uniform suited her strangely well.

“On hot days, lots of customers come because it's cooler here.”

“So you are aiming for CC?”

“Of course. It's business.”

She was blunt.

Yuto ordered an iced coffee, feeling a little intimidated.

“Um… how did you think of it?”

“Mist, shade, and human flow.”

“Human flow?”

“Just being cool is weak. Where people stop, where they wait, where the wind passes—when you combine all of that, the numbers grow.”

The convenience store clerk from yesterday, the bench from earlier, and her words now.

Everything connected.

“…So it really is about combinations.”

“Are you a beginner?”

The woman tilted her head, looking a little amused.

“Well… I basically started yesterday.”

“Then one tip. Early on, improving places where people are troubled by heat gives better numbers than simply cooling hot places.”

“…!”

It felt like being struck in the head.

Not just hot places.

Places where people were troubled.

In other words, context mattered, not just absolute temperature.

“Corners of walkways, waiting lines, in front of benches, air-conditioner exhaust blowback… places like that.”

“Thank you.”

“You're welcome. Oh, but…”

The woman smiled slightly.

“Try not to get yelled at for putting sheets in strange places.”

“…You saw through me.”

“It's written on your face.”

Yuto gave a wry smile as he received the coffee.

A few steps after leaving the shop, his phone vibrated.

“External learning support bonus  
+0.003 CC”

“Even that exists…?”

It was more game-like than ever.

But he was grateful.

His total had passed 0.02 CC.

He was a little more than halfway to the 0.05 goal.

The next problem was whether he could reproduce it elsewhere.

Yuto looked at the station map and searched for candidates.

Places where people stagnated.

Places where heat accumulated.

Places small enough for him to affect.

What caught his eye was a bus stop.

It had a roof.

But the sides were partly enclosed, making heat difficult to escape.

Because a waiting line formed there, people's body heat also collected.

“…There.”

This time, however, he could not just install things without permission.

The station area was conspicuous.

If he did something strange, he would be stopped immediately.

After thinking for a moment, Yuto bought a cheap aluminum sheet and laundry clips at a nearby drugstore.

The cost hurt.

But this was an investment.

Then he approached the staff member in the management booth near the bus stop, trying to look as non-suspicious as possible.

“Excuse me. The sunlight beside this waiting line is pretty harsh. Would it be okay if I attached a small temporary reflector to soften it?”

The staff member looked openly suspicious.

“What is that, filming?”

“No. It's a simple CC heat countermeasure. I'll keep it temporary and out of the way.”

“…Oh, that thing.”

Surprisingly, he understood.

“Five minutes, if it doesn't block traffic. If anyone complains, remove it immediately.”

“Thank you!”

The moment permission was granted, a switch flipped inside Yuto.

He fixed the aluminum sheet temporarily to the side metal frame, only at the angle where direct sunlight entered.

The image was not to block everything, but to soften reflected heat and prevent heat from pooling at the feet of the waiting line.

He also casually placed an empty box where it could serve as a subtle marker, so the end of the line would extend slightly toward the shaded side.

It was a very plain trick.

“Please…”

Several passengers lined up.

One.

Two.

Three.

The position of the line shifted slightly.

Away from the strongest direct sunlight.

At that moment, his smartphone gave a short vibration.

“Waiting comfort improvement detected  
+0.009 CC”

“Yes.”

A few seconds later—

“Heat stagnation reduction detected  
+0.014 CC”

“Oh…!”

It came all at once.

Small, but unmistakable.

And this time, it was not only his own physical sensation.

It was an evaluation of improved waiting conditions for multiple people.

When he understood that, heat rose in his chest.

A positive heat, different from the summer heat.

“I found it…”

The way he could fight in this world.

He had no equipment like a corporation.

No drones, no authority to redesign a city.

But he could find the distortions of heat that troubled people and improve them little by little.

It was not flashy.

But it had value.

“Tutorial Complete  
You achieved 0.05 CC within your living zone.”

A small effect ran across the screen.

For a moment, Yuto could not say anything.

Yesterday's Yuto would have seen 0.05 CC as nothing but a fraction.

But now it was different.

This was not coincidence.

It was not the result of accidentally entering a cool place.

He had found it, thought about it, tested it, and taken the number himself.

“…I did it.”

The moment he said it, it felt real.

Just a little, he had proven he could work within the world.

Then an additional notification appeared.

“Beginner Rank Up: Local Seed  
New Function Unlocked: Nearby Hotspot Sharing”

“Ranks too…?”

Half exasperated, he opened it.

On the map, many small heat-problem spots nearby appeared.

Back alleys, traffic-light waits, pedestrian bridges, corners of shopping streets.

None were large facilities.

They were all places where someone was slightly troubled.

“…I see.”

From here, things would spread further.

Not earning a lot in one place, but accumulating small improvements.

Beyond that, there must be a more complex world that only higher-ranked players could see.

And now, he had taken the first step into it.

Yuto looked up at the sky.

It was still hot.

The sun was merciless.

The city still held heat.

But it was different from yesterday.

This was not a world where he could only endure.

At least under these rules, heat had a way to be read.

“Next time… I'll get more.”

He muttered to no one.

A small success changes a person.

Yesterday's Yuto would have complained about the heat and ended there.

Now he was different.

Inside the heat, he could see targets to攻略.

Inside social distortions, he could see room for intervention.

And most of all, he had learned that there was still something he could do.

His hand tightened slightly around the phone.

In the corner of the screen was today's total.

“Today Earned: 0.057 CC”

Only that much.

But it was not zero.

And sometimes, the fact that something is not zero becomes a great hope.

That was when a new notification slid down from the top of the screen.

“A nearby notable player viewed your activity.”

“…Huh?”

Yuto stopped moving.

The moment he tried to open the details, the notification disappeared.

Only one line remained in the history.

“Viewer: Private  
Rank Tier: Silver or higher”

“…Who?”

Someone was watching a beginner's tiny activity.

Was it coincidence?

Just a system notification?

Or did it mean something else?

He did not know.

But one thing was certain.

This game was truly not single-player.
